\section{Motivation}

Darts is a sports activity that combines elements of accuracy, focus, and social interaction. Millions worldwide participate in the sport in both professional settings and amateur settings such as pubs, clubs, and homes. The digital revolution has transformed many aspects of the recreational sports arena to improve the experience through data collection and automated systems. However, the sport of darts in the amateur arena is still largely in the analog realm in terms of the gameplay process.

\subsection{Challenges in Amateur Dart Scoring}

\subsubsection{Lack of Affordable Automatic Systems}

Lack of Affordable Automatic Scoring Solution for Steel Tip Darts
Apart from the soft tip dart systems that have an electronic board as an inherent component of the system, there is no other affordable automatic scoring solution for the traditional steel tip dart. Although the electronic soft tip dartboard has the advantage of inbuilt hit detection capabilities, it changes the fundamental nature of the gameplay experience. Thus, it is not acceptable as an alternative for players seeking the authentic experience that the classical bristle dartboard provides. Although there are high-end commercial systems such as \textit{\textbf{Scolia}} and \textit{\textbf{Autodarts}} that have the advantage of automatic scoring for steel tip darts, they are quite expensive compared to the traditional dartboard. The reason for the increased price is that they need multiple camera systems with permanent fixtures as well as specialized hardware components. Thus, they are not for the amateur dart enthusiast but for the training facilities or semi-professionals.

\subsubsection{Limitations of Manual Score Entry}

The most popular alternative to the automated detection system in steel tip darts is the manual scoring system. There are different forms that the manual scoring system may take. For example, the player may use a calculator to perform the calculations or may use the mobile application to perform the calculations. Finally, the scoring system may take the form where the player simply writes the points scored on the whiteboard or the blackboard. Regardless of the form that the scoring system takes, the fact remains that the player is required to interrupt the game at regular intervals to update the score after each turn. Such interruption is detrimental to the focus required in the game. The interruption also affects the natural flow of the game. The fact that the player is required to switch between motor skills and mental calculations slows down the natural flow of the game. The fact that the scoring system is manual means that the system is highly prone to errors. Such errors may occur during the checkout calculations. Finally, the scoring system does not have the ability to collect data regarding the player's performance. As sports analytics show, the provision of statistics regarding the player's performance is highly motivating to the players. Thus, the fact that the system does not have the ability to collect data regarding the player's performance means that the players are unable to benefit from the statistics that are provided in most sports environments.
